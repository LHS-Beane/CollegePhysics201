II. Linear Kinematics (1D & 2D) click to toggle
Equation Variables & Units Meaning / Notes
\( v = \dfrac{dx}{dt} \) \(x\): position (m) \(v\): velocity (m/s) Definition of instantaneous velocity.
\( a = \dfrac{dv}{dt} \) \(a\): acceleration (m/s\(^2\)) \(v\): velocity (m/s) Definition of instantaneous acceleration.
\( x = x_0 + v_0 t + \tfrac12 a t^2 \) \(x_0\): initial position (m) \(v_0\): initial velocity (m/s) \(a\): constant acceleration (m/s\(^2\)) Position for constant acceleration.
\( v = v_0 + a t \) Same variables as above. Velocity update for constant acceleration.
\( v^2 = v_0^2 + 2a(x - x_0) \) Eliminates time \(t\). Constant acceleration only.
Projectile: \( x(t) = v_0\cos\theta_0\, t \) \( y(t) = y_0 + v_0\sin\theta_0\, t - \tfrac12 g t^2 \) \(v_0\): launch speed (m/s) \(\theta_0\): launch angle \(g\): 9.8 m/s\(^2\) Neglects air resistance; constant downward acceleration.
III. Vectors & Dot Product click to toggle
Equation Variables Meaning
\( \vec A = A_x\,\hat i + A_y\,\hat j(+A_z\,\hat k) \) \(A_x, A_y, A_z\): components \(\hat i,\hat j,\hat k\): unit vectors Component form in Cartesian coordinates.
\( |\vec A| = \sqrt{A_x^2 + A_y^2 (+A_z^2)} \) Magnitude of a vector. Euclidean length.
\( \vec A \cdot \vec B = A_x B_x + A_y B_y (+A_z B_z) \) Components of vectors \(\vec A, \vec B\). Dot product; equals \(AB\cos\theta\).
\( \vec A \cdot \vec B = AB\cos\theta \) \(A, B\): magnitudes; \(\theta\): angle between them. Useful for work: \(W = \vec F\cdot \vec d\).
IV. Forces & Newton’s Laws click to toggle
Equation Variables Meaning / Notes
\( \sum \vec F = m\vec a \) \(m\): mass (kg) \(\vec a\): acceleration (m/s\(^2\)) Newton’s 2nd Law; vector equation.
\( \vec w = m\vec g \) Weight: force of gravity near Earth’s surface. \(\vec g\) ≈ \((0,-9.8)\,\text{m/s}^2\).
\( f_s \le \mu_s N \) (static friction) \( f_k = \mu_k N \) (kinetic friction) \(N\): normal force (N) \(\mu_s,\mu_k\): coefficients (unitless) Static friction adjusts up to a max; kinetic is usually smaller.
\( F_\text{spring} = -kx \) \(k\): spring constant (N/m) \(x\): displacement from equilibrium (m) Hooke’s Law; minus sign pulls back toward equilibrium.
V. Work, Energy & Power click to toggle
Equation Variables Meaning / Notes
\( W = \vec F\cdot \vec d \) \(\vec d\): displacement (m) Work by a constant force.
\( W = \int_{x_i}^{x_f} F(x)\,dx \) Variable force along \(x\). Area under \(F\)-vs-\(x\) graph.
\( K = \tfrac12 mv^2 \) Translational kinetic energy. Always \(\ge 0\).
\( U_g = mgh \) Gravitational potential (near Earth). Valid for small height changes; depends on choice of zero level.
\( U_s = \tfrac12 kx^2 \) Spring potential energy. \(x\) measured from spring’s natural length.
\( K_i + U_i + W_\text{nc} = K_f + U_f \) \(W_\text{nc}\): work by non-conservative forces. General energy accounting.
\( P = \dfrac{dW}{dt} \), \( P = \vec F\cdot \vec v \) Power (J/s = W). Instantaneous rate of doing work.
VI. Momentum & Collisions click to toggle
Equation Variables Meaning
\( \vec p = m\vec v \) Linear momentum (kg·m/s). Vector quantity; direction of motion.
\( \vec J = \int \vec F\,dt = \Delta \vec p \) Impulse; area under \(F\)-vs-\(t\) graph. Changes momentum.
\( \sum \vec p_\text{initial} = \sum \vec p_\text{final} \) Isolated system (no external net impulse). Momentum conservation.
Elastic 1D: \(v_{1f}, v_{2f}\) from momentum + energy Uses both \(p\) and \(K\) conservation. Perfectly elastic collisions keep total \(K\) constant.
VII. Rotation, Torque & Rotational Energy click to toggle
Equation Variables Meaning / Notes
\( \omega = \dfrac{d\theta}{dt} \), \( \alpha = \dfrac{d\omega}{dt} \) \(\theta\): angle (rad) \(\omega\): angular velocity (rad/s) \(\alpha\): angular acceleration (rad/s\(^2\)) Angular analogs of \(v\) and \(a\).
\( \omega = \omega_0 + \alpha t \) \( \theta = \theta_0 + \omega_0 t + \tfrac12 \alpha t^2 \) \( \omega^2 = \omega_0^2 + 2\alpha(\theta - \theta_0) \) Constant \(\alpha\) only. Rotational kinematics.
\( v = r\omega, \quad a_t = r\alpha, \quad a_c = r\omega^2 \) \(r\): distance from axis (m). Links linear and angular motion.
\( I = \sum m_i r_i^2 \), \( I = \int r^2\,dm \) Moment of inertia about an axis. Measures resistance to changes in rotational motion.
\( K_\text{rot} = \tfrac12 I\omega^2 \) Rotational kinetic energy. Combine with translational \(K\) for rolling.
\( \vec \tau = \vec r \times \vec F \) \( \tau = rF\sin\theta \) Torque about an axis. CCW often chosen positive.
\( \sum \tau = I\alpha \) Rotational analog of \( \sum F = ma \). Newton’s 2nd Law for rotation.
\( W = \int \tau\,d\theta \), \( P = \tau\omega \) Work and power in rotational motion. Analogous to \( W = \int F\,dx\), \( P = Fv\).
Rolling without slipping: \( v_\text{CM} = R\omega \) \(R\): radius of rolling body. Pure rolling (no slipping) condition.
VIII. Oscillations & SHM click to toggle
Equation Variables Meaning / Notes
\( x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \phi) \) \(A\): amplitude (m) \(\omega\): angular frequency (rad/s) \(\phi\): phase (rad) General SHM solution.
\( v(t) = -A\omega\sin(\omega t + \phi) \) \( a(t) = -A\omega^2\cos(\omega t + \phi) = -\omega^2 x(t) \) Velocity and acceleration in SHM. Acceleration is proportional to displacement, opposite direction.
\( \omega = \sqrt{\dfrac{k}{m}} \) for mass–spring \(k\): spring constant (N/m). SHM angular frequency for horizontal mass–spring.
Energy: \( E = \tfrac12 kA^2 = K + U \) \( K = \tfrac12 k(A^2 - x^2) \) \( U = \tfrac12 kx^2 \) Total energy constant for ideal SHM. Energy swaps between kinetic and potential.
