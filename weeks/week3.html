Interactive Panel ‚Äì Block on an Incline (Newton‚Äôs 2nd Law with Friction)

1. Static Friction Threshold ‚Äì Predict & Test

Set initial velocity = 0 and slowly increase the incline angle until the block just begins sliding.

  1. Record the critical angle: \( \theta_{\text{crit}} = \_\_\_\_^\circ \).
  2. Use Newton‚Äôs Second Law at impending motion: \[ mg\sin\theta_{\text{crit}} = \mu_s mg\cos\theta_{\text{crit}} \] Solve for \( \mu_s \).
  3. Compare your result to the slider value for \( \mu_s \).

2. Newton‚Äôs 2nd Law on an Incline

Choose mass \(m\), angle \( \theta \), and kinetic friction \( \mu_k \). Predict acceleration:

\[ a = g(\sin\theta - \mu_k \cos\theta) \]

  1. Compute your predicted acceleration.
  2. Start the simulation and compare.
  3. Explain any mismatch (units, angle reading, friction direction).

3. Changing Gravity ‚Äì Earth, Moon, Mars

Switch between \( g = 9.8, 3.7, 1.6\ \text{m/s}^2 \).

  1. Record acceleration for all three gravity values.
  2. Explain why friction depends on gravity: \[ f_k = \mu_k mg\cos\theta. \]
  3. Which gravity gives the largest acceleration, and why?

4. Energy Loss to Friction

Give the block an upward initial velocity and watch it slow to a stop.

  1. Predict stopping distance using: \[ \Delta KE = -mg d\sin\theta - \mu_k mg\cos\theta\ d. \]
  2. Measure stopping distance in the simulation.
  3. Compare and explain differences.
